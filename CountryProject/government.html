    • Government

    The United States has a democratic government. There are 3 branches of the federal government, Judicial, Executive, and Legislative. Each state has its own government, and each county has its own local government.

    • In the USA, the highest court is the United States Supreme Court, with 9 justices that serve for life. These justices are selected by the President, and are approved by Congress.

    • The United States Congress has 2 parts, the House of Representatives, and the Senate. The House of Representatives has 435 members, each states representatives based on population.

    • The Senate has 100 members, 2 for each of the 50 states. Congress(wo)men write federal laws which must be approved by the House and Senate. Additionally, the President may veto any bill.

    • Lastly, the Executive branch consists of the President, Vice President, and the President's Cabinet. The Cabinet is selected by the President, and approved by Congress.
