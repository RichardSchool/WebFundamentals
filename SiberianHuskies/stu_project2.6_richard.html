Siberian Huskies: Well Adapted for the Snow!

Siberian Huskies are well suited for the snow. They have fur between the pads of their paws to protect their paws from freezing. There is fur inside their ears and their ears can bend back until they are snug against their head. Siberian Huskies have very busy tails that are used to protect their eyes and nose while they sleep. While sleeping, Huskies curl up in a ball, using their tails to cover their faces. Huskies even wear two coats (including a downy undercoat) of fur to keep them warm!

According to the American Kennel Club, Siberian Huskies are famous for having:

  1. Amazing endurance
  2. Eagerness to work
  3. An agreeable temperament

Siberian huskies are known for their top performances as sled dogs. Some sledding terms you may find interesting are:

Musher
The person in control of the sled team
Lead Dog
The dog who leads the rest of the sled team and holds the position first in line
